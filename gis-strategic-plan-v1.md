# Executive Summary

The GIS Team is transforming from a reactive service function into a strategic enabler of data-driven decision-making across Pilbara Ports Authority. This plan outlines our path to reducing reactive work from an estimated 60% to 40% by end of 2026, while delivering critical platform modernization, data quality improvements, and automation initiatives that directly support port operations, safety, and strategic planning.

**Key Goals for 2026:**
- Complete Geocortex ‚Üí VertiGIS transition
- Establish planning & prioritization framework
- Deliver underground services flagship capability
- Implement targeted automation (marine data, weekly triage, strategic planning)
- Reduce reactive work to 50% by mid-year, 40% by year-end

**Strategic Themes:**
1. **Business & Foundation** - Data quality, standards, governance
2. **Systems & Innovation** - Platform modernization, automation, 3D capabilities
3. **Engagement & Capacity** - Stakeholder alignment, training, executive visibility

# 1. Vision & Mission

## Organizational Vision
*"Our ports connect the possibilities of the Pilbara to the world so our customers and communities thrive."*

## Geospatial Vision
**Enable 'Smart Port' planning, operations and marine services via GIS collaboratively providing 'one' spatial view for PPA staff, customers and community.**

PPA to be able to visualise all Port activities as one, enhancing PPA's decisions and driving better outcomes for the Pilbara.

## Mission Statement
**Facilitate the development, maintenance and access of comprehensive spatial data and technology for PPA staff to be able to visualise all Port activities from one single point of truth.**

## Guiding Principles
- **Collaborative** - Work across boundaries
- **Engaged** - Active stakeholder partnerships
- **Immersive** - Rich spatial experiences
- **Integrated** - Connected systems and data
- **Innovative** - Embrace new approaches

- Rollback plan prepared
- Extensive communication and training
- ICT support secured

**Impact:** High (delivery delays, burnout)  
**Likelihood:** Medium-High

- Automation to free capacity
- Request system to control intake
- Regular workload monitoring via WF-01
- Stakeholder expectation management

**Status:** Active management, automation helping

**Impact:** Medium-High (blocks initiatives)  
**Likelihood:** Medium

- Alternative approaches where possible
- Executive escalation path if needed
